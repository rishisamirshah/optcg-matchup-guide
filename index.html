Blue/Red Whitebeard Ace deck built around a 6000-power leader that walls naked 5000 swings, a -2000 leader debuff fired on opponent attacks, and a Newgate (OP13-042) midgame bomb that draws 2 and attaches 2 DON to your attackers.
Ace is one of the few 6000-power leaders in the format, which changes the math entirely: anything attacking at 4000 or 5000 fails naturally per the tie rule (attacker needs >= defender + counter), so most opponents wasting an early raw-leader swing accomplishes nothing. The leader's -2000 debuff (printed text says +2000 but the simulator confirms -2000, fired 2,652 times) shrinks a 7000-9000 swing into something Ace can wall. The DON!! x1 passive draws 1 on damage taken, which means eating a 6000 swing is often a profitable trade. Threat density comes from OP13-042 Newgate, PRB02-008 Marco, OP07-051 Hancock (SP), and OP09-118 Roger as a finisher.
⚙ Global pilot principles (apply to every matchup)
  • Ace's leader is 6000 base power — UNUSUAL for a leader. Per the tie rule (attacker wins ties: attacker >= defender + counter), opponent swings at 4000 or 5000 FAIL into Ace naturally. Don't burn counters on them. Only 6000+ swings threaten Ace's life.
  • Ace's leader effect ALWAYS debuffs opp Leader or Character by -2000 (observed 2,652 times in logs, top targets OP15-058 Enel 421x, OP11-041 Nami 227x, OP15-118 Enel character 168x). TCGcsv text wrongly shows +2000 — missing the minus sign. Spend the -2000 debuff on attackers that almost win the math, not on attackers that already failed Ace 6000 naturally.
  • The DON!! x1 passive draws 1 card on damage taken AND on KO of your 6000+ base-power character (2,473 emissions). Taking a 6000 leader hit is often profitable when you have Newgate/Yamato/Otama in hand — the draw helps you find them.
  • Newgate (OP13-042 SP) is the engine. 10-cost 12000 blocker; Draw 2 + Trash 1 + attaches 2 DON to leader and 1 character. Land Newgate T5-T6 every game.
  • Combat ties go to the attacker per the >= rule. A 6000 swing into Ace 6000 HITS. Counter with +1000 (Jozu, Atmos, Yamato, Garp) to push Ace to 7000 > 6000 = fail. For 10000+ swings (OP15-118 Enel character, OP14-119 Mihawk character, EB02-052 Enel SP) stack Otama +2000 + ST23-001 +2000 + Ace's -2000 leader effect to win the math.
  • OP15-118 (Enel character) is effect-immune at 6 DON or less. Never spend Jozu bounce or Hancock deck-bottom on it. Only Ace's -2000 leader debuff applies as a combat reduction (not effect removal) — combat KO is the only way to remove it (push above 10000 power).
  • Sky-Island Luffy (OP15-098) leader's Pre-* replacement effect refunds your removal of Sky Island 6000+ characters as a free life-to-hand for them. Don't bounce or KO their Wyper or OP15-119 Luffy character — attack the leader directly instead.
