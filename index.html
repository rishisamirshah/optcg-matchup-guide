Portgas D. AceOP13-002

RedBlue3 LifeDON cap 10Aggro / race
A Blue/Red Whitebeard-Pirates aggro deck with 3 Life that races on raw leader damage while converting every life-loss into +1 card via the [DON!! x1] passive, then closes through cheated-out OP13-042 SP Newgate and OP15-020 Fire Fist burn.
Ace exists to punish slow setup decks before they stabilize. You start at 3 Life (the lowest leader in the format) but you also have the highest hand-velocity engine, so your job is to push leader damage early, convert every life-loss into a draw, and try to flatline the opponent before their late-game piece (OP15-118 Enel, OP13-042 Whitebeard, ST24-004 Law & Bepo, EB02-052 Enel SP) lands. You should pilot Ace when the meta is heavy on slow Sky-Island / Mihawk control mirrors and light on Yellow refill decks; you should NOT pilot it into a field full of Luffy OP15-098 Sky-Island, which buries you under Life-replacement value.
⚙ Global pilot principles (apply to every matchup)
  • NEVER attack into OP15-118 Enel with anything less than 10001 power — combat-KO requires above-defender, and OP15-118 with 6-or-less opp DON is permanently 10000 effective (rules_truth.md sec.1). Only OP13-042 Newgate at 12000 reliably KOs it.
  • NEVER use Ace's leader Effect 1 (trash → opp +2000) defensively. It BUFFS the opponent. The only legitimate use is bumping a 6000-base opp character across the 8000+ threshold to open EB04-007 Zoro's [Rush:Character] gate.
  • ALWAYS deploy a tutor on T1 (OP13-016 Garp, ST22-002 Izo, or OP13-043 Otama). Never pass T1 — Ace's 3-Life starting position means you must build hand velocity from turn 1.
  • ALWAYS keep OP15-020 Fire Fist in hand as a +7000 counter unless the matchup has a removable big-body target (Nami's Newgate, Mihawk's Law & Bepo, Lucy's Sabo). Against OP15-118 the KO clause is BLOCKED but the +7000 counter remains the deck's best single-card defense.
  • ALWAYS prefer attacking the opp LEADER over their characters. Ace has limited efficient character removal — life damage from leader-hits both reduces them toward 0 AND triggers your [DON!! x1] passive draw on the way back. Exception: kill engine pieces (P-096 vs Nami, OP13-031 vs Mihawk, OP15-118 only via Newgate vs Enel).
  • NEVER attack 6000+ base Sky-Island characters with the intent to KO them when the Sky-Island Luffy leader (OP15-098) is across the table — every removal attempt triggers PreKO replacement (rules_truth.md sec.8) and refunds him a Life card. Skip those bodies and race the leader instead.
  • ALWAYS preserve OP13-043 Otama (+2000 counter) and ST23-001 Uta (+2000) until T5+ when opp's lethal swings come. The +2000s are precious because Ace runs few of them; +1000 counters (Garp, Yamato, Izo trashed) are the early-turn fuel.

Always pick 1st. The 17-point WR gap is the single largest first/second split in any Ace matchup. Going first you skip their T1 leader-DON-attach, you attack on T2 before they can deploy OP15-118 on T3, and you reach Life 0 on the opponent's side first. What to expect
Enel's whole plan is: drop cheap vassals (OP15-061 Ohm, OP15-067 Shura, OP15-066 Satori) on T1-T2 to chip and stack 1k counters, slam OP15-118 on T3, then grind life-trade with a 6-DON-capped DON deck (see rules_truth.md sec.6) that keeps the protection clause permanently active. Their swings come in many small 5000-6000 power chunks, not one big swing. Late game they pivot to OP10-067 Senor Pink to recycle Events and OP07-064 Sanji as a discounted 6k blocker.
Swing pattern overview
T2 they swing roughly once (leader at 5000) which you can take or counter; T3-T5 the volume swells to 2-3 swings per turn at 4000-6000 power each, with the OP15-118 swing leading at 8000 (10000 with the +2000 clause active). T6-T7 they finish with 3 swings averaging 6000-8000, occasionally pushing one swing to 10000+ via vassal DON-attach. Most swings can be ignored if you have 1 life of slack; the OP15-118 swing is the one to evaluate every single turn.
