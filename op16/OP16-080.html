Marshall.D.Teach (OP16-080)

Black/Yellow Blackbeard Pirates leader with 4 life and 5000 power, built around the Trigger-discard redirect (trash 1 [Trigger] from hand to change an opponent's attack target to leader or a Blackbeard Pirates character) and the +1 cost passive on your characters during opponent's turn. The deck floods cheap Trigger-counter bodies (OP09-095 Laffitte, OP16-104, OP16-108, OP16-119) and closes with OP09-093 Marshall.D.Teach (10c, 12000) and OP15-114 Wyper (5c, 6000).
Teach is a behaviorally split deck — 35.8% going first (491g) and 59.2% going second (655g). Across all 1,537 games it's a 49.3% deck overall, but the play-order swing is huge (-23.4 percentage points). The 4 life cap means every redirect matters; the leader effect lets you spend a [Trigger] hand card to dodge any single attack per turn, which converts hand depth directly into life. The 5000 leader power means 5000 swings HIT via tie and 4000 swings FAIL naturally — most opp leaders are 5000 so leader-vs-leader is symmetric. Champion deck (74g, 40.5% WR) leans hard on OP16-108 (~4 copies, 99.2% of decks), OP09-093 Teach (3.67 avg), OP16-119 (3.92), and OP09-095 Laffitte (3.88).
