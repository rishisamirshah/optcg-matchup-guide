Yamato (079) (OP16-079)

Black mono-color Land of Wano leader with 5 life and 5000 power, built around the trash-recursion loop: when a {Land of Wano} character is played from trash it gains [Rush] this turn, so the deck floods the trash with discard/cycle effects then redeploys with Rush attackers. Champion deck (69g, 47.8% WR) leans on OP16-098 Yamato (6c, 5000, 1000 counter, 4.00 avg copies in 99.7% of decks), OP16-082 Kin'emon (4c, 6000, 96.9%/3.87 avg), OP16-085 Kouzuki Momonosuke (9c, 6000, 96.9%/3.78 avg), and the OP16-091/096/097 character package (98-100% inclusion).
Yamato (079) is a behaviorally split deck — going first is 41.7% over 386 games, going second is 54.4% over 445 games, a 12.7-percentage-point swing. Across all 1,113 games it's 49.5% overall, so it's a coin-flip deck whose play-order matters more than matchup spread. The 5 life total gives Yamato 1 extra hit of buffer over Teach (4 life), but the 5000 leader power is symmetric vs every common opponent in this pool — 5000 swings HIT via tie, 4000 swings FAIL naturally, 6000+ swings HIT clearly. The Rush-from-trash leader effect means tempo lives in the discard pile, not the hand, so the deck punishes opp board-clears that try to attrit Yamato.

Plan
In 201 games Yamato pros went 127-74 = 63.2% — this is the best common matchup in the pool. Going first or second, both lanes feed into the same plan. Pros mulligan to find OP16-082 Kin'emon (3.87 avg copies in 96.9% of decks) and OP16-097 (3.93 avg in 100% of decks). Plan: out-life Teach (5 vs their 4), trade trash-recursion bodies into their Trigger-redirect economy, and close T6-T7 with Rush attackers.
What to expect
Teach is a 4-life 5000-power Black/Yellow leader whose passive raises your character costs +1 on Teach's turn and whose leader effect trashes a [Trigger] card from hand to redirect 1 attack per turn. Expect OP09-095 Laffitte (1c, 1000, 1000 counter) on T1, OP16-104/108/119 Trigger-tagged bodies T2-T4, EB04-058 Borsalino (5c, 6000) or OP15-114 Wyper (5c, 6000) T4-T5, and OP09-093 Marshall.D.Teach (10c, 12000) closing T6-T7.
Swing pattern
Yamato 5000 leader vs Teach 5000 leader is symmetric — 5000 swings HIT via tie either direction. Yamato has 5 life to Teach's 4, so the race math is one hit in Yamato's favor. Teach's Trigger-redirect taxes one [Trigger] hand card per swing dodged; force Teach to redirect early by stacking 5000-6000 swings T3-T4 so they exhaust the redirect pool before OP16-085 Momonosuke (9c, 6000, 1000 counter) lands.
Mulligan
Keep openings with OP16-082 Kin'emon (96.9% of decks, 3.87 avg copies) AND a 1-cost Wano body (OP16-091 at 98.9%/3.99 avg or OP16-097 at 100%/3.93 avg) to start filling trash. Mulligan slow hands with only 9-cost OP16-085 Momonosuke — the deck needs T3-T4 development to set up T6 close.
