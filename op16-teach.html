How the top pilots actually play it

❤️ LIFE MANAGEMENT
  • Treat life as ammunition, not a wall. Take the early hits uncountered to bank counter cards and dig pieces, then dig in and defend once you reach the 2-3 life range where your engine is online.
  • Refill life on your own terms. Shiryu OP16-108 and Teach OP16-119 both add a card to the top of your Life on deploy, so you can chip down for value early and then re-bank a point or two mid-game to climb back to a safe 3-4.
  • Do not panic at low life. Teach is built to win comfortably from 1-2 life while the opponent is the one being pushed to 0, so hold your final points behind blockers and counters rather than trading them away early.
  • Know that ZEHAHAHAHA OP16-116 costs you a life (Send 1 Life to Hand) to deploy the 7c Teach OP09-093. Bank that point back beforehand or accept the dip only when the 12000 body and its nullify swing the game.
  • Jesus Burgess OP16-107's 'Send 1 Life to Hand' is a card-advantage tool, not a heal. It lowers your life count to gain a card, so fire it when you need fuel, not when you are trying to stabilize.
📈 SEQUENCING & CURVE
  • Follow the cost-up curve: 1c Laffitte/Van Augur, then Burgess, then 6c Shiryu (8000), then 8c Teach (10000), then 10c Teach (12000). Each step both advances board and grows your tax pressure.
  • Build toward the ZEHAHAHAHA OP16-116 swing: rest 8 DON to deploy the 7c Teach OP09-093 and send a life to hand. This is your defining mid-game tempo turn, so set up DON and a life buffer in advance.
  • Close with attrition, not a single finisher. Clear blockers in order with your big Teach bodies, then dump 8 DON onto a leftover 1c Vasco Shot OP16-110 and swing it as the unanswerable last hit.
  • Sequence your blocker removal before your closer. Kill the first wall with Teach OP16-119 (10000), the next with Teach OP09-093 (12000), then send the loaded small body through the now-open lane.
↩️ REDIRECT (leader effect)
  • Redirect is your repeatable defensive engine, not a one-off. Expect to fire it 2-4 times a game, each paired with trashing a [Trigger] card from hand, so always keep a dead Trigger around as fuel.
  • Aim redirects at your cheap chip bodies (Van Augur OP16-103, Vasco Shot OP16-110, Burgess OP16-107). Sacrificing them turns a big incoming attack into card advantage instead of a dead block.
  • Prefer redirecting onto Van Augur (Draw 1) or Vasco Shot (Draw 1 + rest a rester) so the body you give up still draws and disrupts on its way out.
  • Only burn cards you do not need as redirect fuel. Spend dead [Trigger] copies and spare chip bodies, never something your plan relies on.
  • Situational: at 1 life, you can redirect a lethal swing onto a chip body to protect your final life point. Treat this as a last-ditch save rather than a routine line.
♟ MANEUVERING
  • Choose to go SECOND whenever you hold turn order. The extra card and on-curve Fullalead OP09-099 deploy let you out-resource the opponent rather than race them.
  • Open deploy-and-dig: drop Fullalead OP09-099 and rest it to trash-and-reveal, or double-deploy two 1c Van Augur OP16-103 when you want bodies on board. Get your engine down before you start committing counters.
  • Fire Fullalead OP09-099 nearly every turn as your draw motor. Treat it as a non-negotiable per-turn action that keeps your hand stocked with redirect fuel and counters.
  • Use the 7c Teach OP09-093 as your late-game wall. At 12000 it trades up into and destroys most blockers, and its deploy nullifies the opposing leader or key card, so hold it for the turn it both blocks and disrupts.
🃏 MULLIGAN
  • Keep hands with your dig and life-bank tools. Shiryu OP16-108, Vasco Shot OP16-110, and Catarina OP16-104 alongside a turn-1 dig card give you both an engine and a counter to survive the early hits.
  • Lead turn 1 with a dig spell like This is MY AGE OP09-096 to fix your hand and find Fullalead, then start banking life from there. (Situational: This is MY AGE lead deeply verified in one game.)
  • Look for Catarina OP16-104 as a precise counter. Discarding it for Counter 2000 can push your leader over a 6000 attacker (5000 + 2000 = 7000 > 6000) and force an attack to fail at a key moment.
  • A Teach-heavy clog hand is sometimes a keep, but treat it as the riskier line. (Situational: verified in a single game.)
⚔️ MATCHUP NOTES
  • Against aggressive leaders, lean into the take-damage plan early and let your redirects and 12000 Teach bodies stabilize the mid-game. You can win from 0-1 life as long as your board is winning the race.
  • The 7c Teach OP09-093 deploy nullify is matchup-defining against effect-heavy decks. Save it for the turn it cancels their leader or key payoff (e.g., nullifying an Ace or Newgate) while also blocking.
  • Situational: in one game the deck survived a dip to 0 life, climbed back to 1, and held there through the win. It shows the deck's ceiling for low-life grinds but should not be your default plan.
  • Against control or grindy boards, win by attrition: keep firing Fullalead, keep banking life, and out-resource them until your multi-body alpha plus a loaded chip body closes.
