◆ Role in the meta

Nami is the format's designated Enel hard-counter (69.0% over 976 games): Enel's fast life pressure is exactly the fuel her draw-on-life-loss leader engine wants, so the matchup that should race her instead feeds her. She is a grind-and-stabilize midrange deck that banks card advantage T3-T10 and converts T11-14, favored into Y.Luffy (Monkey.D.Luffy (OP15-098), 64.6%) and roughly even into Ace and Enel-SP. Her weak spots are decks that ignore her life math: Lucy (43.4%) outpaces the grind, Green Luffy (35.3%) goes wide faster than she stabilizes, and Mihawk (39.5%) is the Banish deck that sends her hit life cards to trash so her draw engine and Triggers never fire.

◆ Win conditions

  • Win the card-war: her leader draws on EITHER player's life loss (hand <=7), firing ~4-5 times a game, so you stockpile a hand the opponent cannot match and never run out of counters or threats.
  • Make life renewable, not a countdown: Robin (+2 to Life-top, net +1 Life), Borsalino, Zoro, Kikunojo and Nami053 reload Life-top with deck cards, so you stabilize at 2 life and out-grind from there instead of being raced out.
  • Wall the leader with the +2000 self-buff: trash 1 card (DON on leader) to push Nami to 7000 and deny most 5000-7000 leader pokes for a single card all game.
  • Refill the board faster than they can KO it: Hogback/Perona/Kumacy [On KO]/[Trigger] re-play Thriller Bark characters from trash, so trading into your board often just triggers a revive.
  • Close with the big bodies once stabilized: Gecko Moria (10000) and Newgate (12000 Blocker) as the late wall/threat, Nico Robin (8000), and Sanji granting [Unblockable] to punch the finisher through a blocker.

◆ The core engine — why Nami wins

Nami plays roughly one card per turn and pumps all leftover DON into attacks (+1000 each), letting her develop a body and still race the leader. The deck is three interlocking loops: a draw engine fed by life loss, a heal/refuel engine that reloads Life-top from the deck, and a Thriller Bark revival engine that refills the board from trash. The draw engine pays the heal engine (more cards = more Yellow refuelers), the heal engine restocks Triggers that feed the revival engine, and the revival engine keeps blockers and bodies on board to buy time for the draw engine. Newgate (10c Blocker: draw 2 + ~3.9 rested DON back) is the turn that fuses all three.

◆ Signature lines — the combos winners repeat

The Newgate DON-engine

When: Late game on a 10-DON turn (T9-T12), especially once stabilized at 2 life and needing to wall + threaten.

  1. Spend all 10 DON to play Edward.Newgate (SP) — a 12000 [Blocker].
  2. [On Play]: draw 2, trash 1 (keep the two best; a card like Kikunojo/Newgate is fine to keep as +2000-buff fuel).
  3. Return up to 2 rested DON each to the leader and one character (~3.9 DON back across the board; most often onto the leader, 1416x).
  4. Attach those ~4 returned DON to attackers (+1000 each) and swing twice — typically leader + one body.

→ Payoff: One 10-cost card becomes a 12000 wall + 2 cards + ~4 DON, converting a 'do-nothing' big-DON turn into draw, defense, and two attacks at once.

Heal / refuel line (life as a renewable resource)

When: As you approach 2 life and need to stabilize while restocking Triggers (T5-T11).

  1. Lead with Robin (Nico Robin (055), 7c): trash 1 own Life-top, add 2 from deck — net +1 Life and 2 stacked Triggers, leaving an 8000 body.
  2. Layer Borsalino (its add only fires at <=2 Life) and Zoro/Kikunojo/Nami053 to keep reloading Life-top from the deck (Robin 2571x, Borsalino 713x, Zoro 478x, Kiku 135x).
  3. Each reload also restocks Trigger fuel (Kumacy/Hogback/Perona/Baby5) so flips defend you for free.
  4. Attach all leftover DON to the leader and swing — refueling does not cost you offense.

→ Payoff: Nami stabilizes at 2 life and out-grinds: her 4 life is a renewable resource, and each heal both buys time and reloads the Trigger/revival engines.

◆ Defensive framework — Nami's five tools

Nami defends with five tools, and because her life is renewable (heals reload Life-top) and a TAKE draws her a card, her defensive calculus is more forgiving than other decks. Remember the tie rule: an attack succeeds at attacker_power >= defender_power + counters, so a bare 5000 Nami leader is HIT by a 5000 swing — you must push her strictly above the swing to make it FAIL.

The decision order

  • 1. A 5000 swing into a bare leader HITS (tie -> attacker): either +2000-buff to 7000 (FAIL) or just TAKE it for the draw if you'd rather save the card.
  • 2. Vs 7000: the +2000 buff ALONE ties (still HITS) — add a counter (buff -> 9000 > 7000) only if the life matters this turn; otherwise take the draw.
  • 3. Vs 10000+ (e.g. Enel Enel (OP15-118)): buff + one counter is rarely enough — TAKE it (draw a card) or BLOCK with Borsalino/Newgate.
  • 4. Spend counters to PROTECT life, not to win every clash; never empty your hand of counters into the board.
  • 5. As life nears 2, prioritize a heal/refuel turn (Borsalino/Robin) over an extra attack — stabilize, then out-grind.
  • 6. Banish (Mihawk Dracule Mihawk) overrides all of this: a banished life card goes to trash, so neither your draw nor the life [Trigger] fires — block or KO the Banish attacker, do not 'take for value'.

Letting life crater before a stabilizer is down. — Lost-first games hit median 2 life by T6 (vs median 3 in won-first) and faced 3.77 swings/game; with no Newgate yet, taking 42.9% at 2 life under that volume is fatal. Spending the +2000 buff alone against a 7000 swing. — 5000 + 2000 = 7000 = 7000 TIES, and the tie rule gives the attacker the hit — the buffed swing still connects and you wasted a card. Not deploying a T2 body going second. — Won-second games deployed a 4-cost (Kikunojo/Zoro/Hogback) on T2 via the DON-to-4 ramp; lost-second games leaned on Kikunojo (4/7g) but still hit median 2 life by T7 under 3.86 swings/game. ◆ Winning signs

  • ▲ Your hand is bigger than the opponent's by mid-game — the leader draw has fired 4-5 times and you never run out of counters.
  • ▲ You've stabilized at 2 life with a heal in reserve (Borsalino/Robin), so their clock has stalled while yours keeps drawing.
  • ▲ Your board keeps coming back: every KO of a Thriller Bark body triggers a revive from trash.
  • ▲ Newgate is online — a 12000 Blocker wall plus draw 2 and ~4 DON returned each time you cast it.
  • ▲ You're racing their leader (not their board) and a Moria 10000 / Robin 8000 / unblockable-Sanji line is set up to close T11-14.

◆ Losing signs

  • ▼ You're being out-paced before you stabilize — Lucy/Green Luffy go wide faster than your heals reload (43.4% / 35.3%).
  • ▼ You FORCED a take with no counters in hand — that is the losing-bucket pattern, not a chosen draw.
  • ▼ Your DON sat stacked on one attacker instead of being spread across an extra swing, so you applied less total pressure per turn.
  • ▼ You're against Banish (Mihawk) and life cards are going to trash — no draw, no Triggers, no heals to lean on.
  • ▼ Your hand is empty of counters and the +2000 buff has nothing to trash — the wall is down and 5000 swings start connecting (tie -> attacker).

◆ Global play principles

  1. Point pressure at the leader, but spend on the board when it matters: cumulative T1-T5 both winners and losers send roughly 61-65% of swings at the leader (winners ~61%, losers ~64-65%), and losers actually point at the leader slightly MORE, so 'just race the leader' is not what separates wins from losses — tempo and card-war do.
  2. Pump leftover DON into attacks — Nami plays ~1 card/turn, so every spare DON becomes +1000 on a swing. Throw one more attack per turn and spread DON wide, not stacked.
  3. Treat takes as draws: a TAKE fires the leader engine (hand <=7), so chosen takes are cheap; only FORCED takes with no counters left lose.
  4. Hold the +2000 buff for the swing that matters — it costs a card and DON on the leader, and it ties (not beats) a 7000 swing, so pair it with a counter when you must make 7000 FAIL.
  5. Make life renewable: schedule Borsalino/Robin/Zoro heals to stabilize at 2 and reload Triggers rather than racing.
  6. Let them KO your Thriller Bark bodies — Hogback/Perona/Kumacy revive from trash, so their removal taxes them more than you.
  7. Respect Banish (Mihawk): banished life goes to trash, killing your draw AND the life Trigger — block/KO the Banish attacker instead of taking.
