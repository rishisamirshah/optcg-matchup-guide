Ace vs Purple Enel

how to actually play it
Your characters are supposed to die here. Ace wins through the leader and Newgate SP, not by holding a board. Spend bodies and +1000 counters freely, bank your +2000s and Ace's −2000 for the one card Enel has that beats your wall: Enel OP15-118.
Honest win rate: this is a losing matchup. Play it tight. Across 4,930 ranked Ace-vs-Purple-Enel games the deck wins ~40% overall. The seat matters more than anything: going first is roughly even, going second is the bad side.

◆ Why you're losing (it's not the removal)

Enel's rest / freeze / KO / bounce is loud, but it is not your loss condition. The players who win eat more removal than the players who lose. You lose because you play as if the board matters — rebuilding it, protecting it, spending counters to defend it — when Ace wins through the leader and Newgate, not the board.

Your characters do three jobs, then they're allowed to die:

1 — Counter fuel

Otama +2000 and Uta +2000 plus the +1000s (Jozu, Atmos, Garp, Izo) are worth more in hand than on board. Otama and Jozu are the two most-used counters in the matchup. A card you "kept on board" is a counter you didn't get to use.

2 — The Newgate blocker (the one body that must stick)

Newgate SP — 12000 blocker, Draw 2 + Trash 1, attaches 2 DON to your leader. In 86% of wins, 75% of losses. It is the answer to the OP15-118 wall, not a removal answer. Block the 10000 swing with it and it survives (12000 > 10000).

3 — Marco, a one-shot blocker + on-KO Draw 2

Marco is the single most rest/froze/disabled target Enel has — Enel wants to lock your blocker. That's fine. Marco blocking one swing and drawing 2 when it dies is the card doing its whole job.

The OP15-118 trap you can't solve with removal

Enel's OP15-118 (and its twin OP15-060) get +2000 and effect-immunity while Enel has ≤6 DON. Enel is hard-capped at 6 DON forever, so that's always on. It sits at 10000 effective and swings every turn from T4. You cannot Jozu-bounce it, deck-bottom it, or KO it — every removal attempt fizzles. Two things still work because neither removes it: Ace's leader −2000 combat debuff (drops it to 8000 for that attack) and Hancock's lock (can't attack a turn). The answer to the biggest threat is also not removal — it's a blocker, a turn of lock, or stacked counters with the −2000.

Commit freely vs hold for the wave

HOLD (keep in hand)

  • Every +2000 counter (Otama, Uta) until the OP15-118 wave at T4–T7. Stacked, they're your only cards that beat a 10000 swing.
  • Ace's −2000 leader effect, strictly for the 10000 swing. Do NOT spend it on a 4000–5000 character swing — those fail Ace 6000 for free.
  • Newgate SP — but land it by T5–T6. Don't hold it dead.

COMMIT (spend, expect to lose it)

  • +1000 counters (Jozu, Atmos, Garp, Izo, Yamato-as-counter) on the 6000 swings only.
  • Any tempo body (Hancock, Yamato, Zoro) once — then let Enel remove it. Don't redeploy the same role twice into removal.
  • Your leader, every turn. Ace 6000 (+1–2 DON) into the 5000 Enel leader is a free hit Enel can't out-scale.

◆ Yamato — deploy it, don't guard it

Hard-deploy Yamato almost every time. Ace starts at LIFE 3 and is never above 3, so its "3-or-less life → Draw 2 + DON attach" condition always fires. In won games 100% of deploys happened at ≤3 life, clustering hard on T3 (797 of 1,172 deploys). Attach the DON to Ace for a 7000 leader swing.

Yamato is fuel and a flexible counter, not a centerpiece — played in 51% of wins, 46% of losses, and used as a +1000 in 1,749 won-game swings. The one thing to never do: spend a counter to guard a deployed Yamato. You already cashed the Draw 2 + DON when you played it. Most attacks at it are cheap; let them resolve. Let it die happily to the OP15-118 swing — that 10000 is being spent on your body, not your leader.

Over-guard, illustrated. One lost game saw the player guard a deployed Yamato three times — Jozu on a T3 5000, Garp on another T3 5000, Kingdew on a T5 6000 — burning three counters. Both 5000 swings would have failed into Yamato's 6000 anyway. Then a 9000 swing KO'd it through the block. Three counters spent, Yamato dead, game lost.

Decision rule: (1) Deploy by T3 for the Draw 2 + DON. (2) Hold as a +1000 only if your hand is flush. (3) Never burn a counter to guard it. (4) Let it eat the OP15-118 swing.

◆ Zoro — buff first, swing second, never into the wall

Zoro has no counter value — you can't pitch it. Its job is the +2000 leader buff: Ace 6000 → 8000, swinging 9000–12000 with DON. That's repeatable pressure Enel can't out-scale (stuck at 6 DON). The 9000 body is a fine swing at any target ≤9000 — but never the 10000 wall.

The trap: a raw 9000 body into OP15-118 fails on its face (9000 < 10000), and they hold counters for exactly this swing. One lost game pumped Zoro to 12000 into OP15-118 — the opponent dropped two +2000 counters: 10000 + 2000 + 2000 = 14000 > 12000 = dead body for nothing. To kill OP15-118, use Ace's −2000 debuff on defense, not Zoro: drop it to 8000, then Ace 6000 + Otama +2000 + Uta +2000 = 10000 > 8000 = fail. Keep Zoro's job as pressure.

Decision rule: (1) Default = play for the +2000 leader buff. (2) Swing the 9000 body only at a character ≤9000 or the Enel leader. (3) Never into OP15-118's 10000. (4) Kill the wall with the −2000 + stacked counters on defense, not with Zoro.

◆ Your curve & missing your big drop

Your deck has no real 8-drop. The top end is exactly three cards, and Newgate SP in 86% of wins is the single biggest number in the matchup. When you draw it on time you win; when you brick it you don't lose on the spot — you grind.

Why it's winnable without the bomb: your 6000 leader is a wall

Ace's leader is 6000; Enel's is 5000, and Enel is hard-capped at 6 DON, so he can never out-scale you with DON. His most common attack is leader + 1 DON = 6000 — ties Ace's 6000 and HITS via the ≥ rule, but it's cheap to deny (one +1000) or fine to take on purpose to bank life. His 1-cost characters (Ohm, Shura, Satori, all base 2000) pumped to 4000–5000 fail into your 6000 for free — never counter those. The only thing that beats your wall hard is OP15-118 — and OP15-118 is answerable. That's why you can lose Newgate and stay in the game.

Newgate timing — race it onto the board by T5–T6

Winners cluster Newgate on T4–T5 (most common winning turn: T5, 774 games). Losers skew later or never land it behind enough counters. Holding it for a "better" turn is a top-3 losing mistake — by T7 a winning Ace sits at 1 life going first while a losing Ace has already bled to 0.

When you miss Hancock AND Newgate — the grind plan

  • Lean on the 6000 wall. Counter nothing under 6000. Every 2000–5000 swing fails on its own. Burning a counter (or the −2000) on a 4000 swing is the #2 losing mistake.
  • Ration counters for OP15-118 only. Winners and losers play similar counters — the difference is winners still have them when OP15-118 swings. Counter starvation is how you lose, not the card itself: in losses, every OP15-118 hit went through with zero counters spent.
  • Use the −2000 on the right target, every time. Save it for the 10000 swing. 10000 − 2000 = 8000, then a 2k + a 1k = 9000 > 8000 holds. A single 2k only reaches 8000 = ties = still HITS.
  • Bank life on purpose, don't bleed it. Clearest signal in the data. At T5 winners sit at 2 life (1,126 games); losers are collapsing (827 at 1, 227 already at 0). Take a clean 6000 hit to go 3→2 rather than dump a counter you'll need.
  • Clock with Zoro and late Hancock. You're racing a 6000 leader into a 5000 leader that can't scale — survive the one card (OP15-118) that can, until Newgate's Draw 2 refills you.

◆ The rules that win this matchup

  • 3 life, not 4. Plan from 3 → 2 (hold through T4–T6) → 1 (T7).
  • Never spend on a sub-6000 swing. The 5000 leader and base-2000 1-costs fail into Ace 6000 for free. Losers waste counters here and have nothing for the 10000.
  • Every 6000 swing = exactly one +1000. Use your worst +1000 (Jozu, Atmos, Izo, Garp, Yamato-as-counter). Never a +2000 on a 6000.
  • OP15-118's 10000 is the whole game. Ties go to the attacker, so beat it, don't tie it. −2000 → 8000 then +2000 +1000, Hancock-lock, or Newgate block. Removal never works.
  • Bank Otama & Uta for T4–T7. They exist to beat the 10000, not the 6000s.
  • Land Newgate by T5–T6. 86% of wins vs 75% of losses. Once it blocks the wave, your counters last and you out-grind the frozen 6-DON board.
